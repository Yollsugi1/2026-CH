## Strategic Frameworks (Reusable)

### Framework 1: Magazine Model vs. Performance Model
**When to use:** Aligning expectations with e-commerce clients

**Magazine Model (Old way):**
- High volume, aesthetic content
- Indirect business impact
- Long time to ROI
- Fixed fees regardless of results

**Performance Model (Our way):**
- Conversion-focused content
- Direct sales attribution
- Immediate feedback loop
- Revenue share or performance-based

**Application:** Any commerce-focused brand (fashion, beauty, F&B, lifestyle products)

### Framework 2: Heritage Repositioning
**When to use:** Legacy brands struggling with "old" perception

**Steps:**
1. Identify authentic heritage asset (years, craftsmanship, cultural role)
2. Find contemporary lens (retro, documentary, cultural commentary)
3. Target younger demographic with positioning
4. Create visual/content system that bridges eras

**Application:** Any brand 10+ years old competing with newer players

### Framework 3: Scene-Based Strategy (막걸리 현장 Model)
**When to use:** Brands that want authentic, not advertising, feel

**Core Principle:** Show scenes/moments where product naturally exists, don't promote

**Three Content Types:**
- Making scene (behind production)
- Consuming scene (people using/enjoying)
- Discovery scene (outsider perspective)

**Application:** Any brand with strong cultural/lifestyle component

### Framework 4: Retro Visual System
**When to use:** Brands with 20-30 year heritage, targeting MZ

**Components:**
- Visual language (90s-Y2K aesthetic)
- Film look cinematography
- Nostalgic music/sound design
- Contemporary execution of retro style

**Application:** Heritage F&B, lifestyle brands, any Korean brand from 90s-2000s
