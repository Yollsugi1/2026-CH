# Quotation Builder

Systematic pricing and quotation generation for Creatorhood services. Creates accurate, professional quotes based on service scope, client type, and engagement model.

## When to Use

**MUST USE when:**
- User says "견적", "quotation", "quote", "pricing", "how much"
- Creating proposals that need pricing
- Discussing budget or investment
- User asks "what does it cost?"
- Preparing sales materials with pricing

**USE PROACTIVELY when:**
- User describes project scope without mentioning price
- Discussing deliverables (helps estimate cost)
- Client type suggests specific pricing model
- User needs to compare service options
- Budget conversation imminent

## Core Pricing Philosophy

**Value-Based, Not Cost-Plus:**
- Price based on value delivered to client
- Consider their business size and goals
- Account for strategic vs. execution work
- Flexibility for alignment (performance models)

**Transparent, Not Hidden:**
- Clear breakdown of what's included
- Explicit about what's NOT included
- No surprise fees
- Options at different price points

**Flexible, Not Rigid:**
- Multiple engagement models
- Scalability (start small, grow)
- Performance-based options (e-commerce)
- Project vs. retainer options

## Quotation Process

### Step 1: Discovery

**Gather information:**
- What's the client's business? (Category, size, stage)
- What's the primary goal? (Awareness, sales, repositioning)
- What's the current situation? (Social presence, team, budget)
- What's the scope? (Content types, platforms, volume)
- What's the timeline? (Start date, duration, urgency)

### Step 2: Match to Model

**Decision tree:**

```
Is it e-commerce with direct sales tracking?
├─ YES → Performance Partnership (Model 2)
└─ NO → Continue

Do they need full strategy + execution?
├─ YES → Strategic Partnership (Model 1)
└─ NO → Continue

Do they have strategy, need execution only?
├─ YES → Content Production (Model 3)
└─ NO → Continue

Do they have team, need strategy only?
├─ YES → Strategy Consulting (Model 4)
└─ NO → Maybe Starter Package (Model 5)

Budget < ₩2M/month?
└─ YES → Starter Package (Model 5)
```

### Step 3: Calculate Quote

**Base service model:** ₩[X]M per month

**Customize based on:**
- Deliverable volume (more/less than standard)
- Complexity (premium production, multiple platforms)
- Strategic intensity (foundation month, ongoing)
- Client size (larger brands = higher pricing capability)
- Competitive landscape (what others charge)

**Add-ons:** + ₩[Y]K for additional services

**Discounts:** - [Z]% for commitment or package

**Total:** ₩[Final] per month

### Step 4: Create Proposal

**Quotation should include:**

1. **Executive Summary**
   - What they're getting (outcomes)
   - Investment amount
   - Timeline

2. **Scope of Work**
   - Specific deliverables (quantity, type)
   - What's included
   - What's NOT included (clear boundaries)

3. **Pricing Breakdown**
   - Base service: ₩[X]
   - Add-ons: ₩[Y]
   - Discounts: -₩[Z]
   - Total: ₩[A]

4. **Timeline**
   - Month 1: Foundation work
   - Months 2-X: Ongoing delivery
   - Key milestones

5. **Payment Terms**
   - When invoiced (1st of month, after delivery, etc.)
   - Payment method
   - Late payment policy

6. **Terms & Conditions**
   - Cancellation policy
   - IP and usage rights
   - Confidentiality
   - Revision limits

### Step 5: Present Options

**Always give 2-3 options:**

**Option A: Full Service** (Recommended)
- Everything they need
- ₩[X]M per month

**Option B: Core Package**
- Essential services
- ₩[Y]M per month

**Option C: Performance-Based** (if applicable)
- Revenue share model
- ₩[Base] + [%] of sales

**Why options?**
- Gives client choice and control
- Anchors price (Option A makes B seem reasonable)
- Shows flexibility
- Most clients choose middle option

## Special Pricing Situations

### Startup / Early-Stage

**Considerations:**
- Limited budget but need help
- May scale up if successful
- Higher risk of failure

**Approach:**
- Starter Package (₩1.5-2.5M)
- Performance models when applicable
- 3-month pilot before long commitment
- Upgrade path defined

### Enterprise / Large Brand

**Considerations:**
- Larger budgets available
- More stakeholders and process
- Higher expectations
- Longer sales cycles

**Approach:**
- Premium end of range (₩4-6M+)
- Comprehensive scope
- Dedicated team mentions
- Detailed reporting and accountability

### Non-Profit / Social Impact

**Considerations:**
- Limited budgets
- Good cause
- Potential for portfolio/PR value

**Approach:**
- 20-30% discount from standard
- Pro-bono only if strategic value
- Clear boundaries on scope
- Still professional standards

### Friends / Network Referrals

**Considerations:**
- Relationship value
- Word-of-mouth potential
- But need to maintain business professionalism

**Approach:**
- 10-15% "network rate"
- Not free (devalues work)
- Clear that it's special consideration
- Written agreement still important

## Pricing Psychology & Presentation

### How to Frame Price

**DON'T say:**
"It costs ₩3.5M per month"

**DO say:**
"The investment is ₩3.5M per month, which includes [specific value]. Based on [case study], you can expect [outcomes], which translates to [business impact]."

### Anchoring

**Start high, offer options:**
- Show premium option first (₩5M)
- Makes standard option (₩3.5M) seem reasonable
- Budget option (₩2M) available if needed

### Value Translation

**Connect price to business outcomes:**
- "₩3.5M per month = ₩117K per day for complete social presence"
- "That's less than one full-time employee salary, and you get entire team"
- "If we achieve [X sales/awareness], the ROI is [Y]x"

### Payment Terms

**Monthly retainer:**
- Invoice 1st of month
- Net 15 payment terms
- Auto-renewal with 30-day notice

**Project-based:**
- 50% deposit to start
- 50% upon completion
- Milestones for larger projects

**Performance-based:**
- Monthly reconciliation
- Payment within 15 days of invoice
- Transparent tracking report

## Common Pricing Questions & Answers

### "Why so expensive?"

**Response:**
"I understand ₩[X]M feels significant. Let me break down what you're getting... [itemize value]. This isn't just content creation—it's [strategic value]. Compared to hiring in-house [calculate], or working with traditional agency [compare], our approach provides [specific advantages]."

### "Can you go lower?"

**Response:**
"I want to be transparent about our pricing. We've calculated this based on [scope and value]. Where we have flexibility is [options]: we can adjust scope, start with smaller package, or explore performance model for e-commerce. What matters most to you?"

### "What's included?"

**Response:**
"Great question. Here's exactly what you get... [detailed list]. Equally important, here's what's NOT included... [exclusions]. This clarity helps us both stay aligned on expectations."

### "How does this compare to others?"

**Response:**
"Most traditional agencies charge ₩[X]-[Y]M for similar scope. We're [competitive/premium/value] because [differentiation]. The difference is [value proposition]. We're transparent about our pricing rather than hiding costs."

### "Can we start smaller?"

**Response:**
"Absolutely. We have a Starter Package at ₩[X]M that includes [reduced scope]. It's a great way to test the partnership with lower commitment. You can always scale up to full service."

## Pricing Decision Checklist

Before finalizing quote, verify:

□ Matched to appropriate service model
□ Deliverables clearly defined (quantity, type, platform)
□ What's included vs. excluded is explicit
□ Timeline and milestones specified
□ Payment terms clear
□ Competitive but sustainable pricing
□ Options provided (not single price)
□ Value translated to business outcomes
□ Discounts applied appropriately
□ Room for negotiation if needed

## Critical Reminders

**Pricing Principles:**
1. Never undervalue work to win client
2. Be transparent, not apologetic
3. Provide options, empower choice
4. Connect price to value and outcomes
5. Document everything in writing
6. Leave room for healthy negotiation
7. Know when to walk away

**Red Flags:**
- Client only cares about cheapest price
- Asks for "free trial" beyond reasonable scope
- Unclear budget or decision-making authority
- Wants everything but pays nothing
- Disrespects your pricing from start

**Remember:** Pricing reflects value. If we undercharge, we devalue our work and can't deliver quality. Sustainable pricing = sustainable partnerships = better results.
