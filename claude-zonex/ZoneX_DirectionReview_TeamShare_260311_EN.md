## 0. Why We Did a Full Direction Review

### What the Original Concept Was Aiming For

**High-level concept**: "Street Woman Fighter (SWF) for physical training" / "SWF but competing in cardio"

- Market gap: "The *doing* market (HYROX/CrossFit) is exploding, but the *watching* market is empty"
- Positioning: Not broadcast (Physical: 100), not competition (HYROX) — "Team-based seasonal sports entertainment IP"
- Structure: 8 teams × cardio missions × 10-week season league
- References: Kings League, Black Combat, DRL

Sehan's A+B=C framing from the kickoff:
> "Elite crews representing each discipline prove which sport produces the most complete athlete — a cinematic sports survival on the neutral battleground of cardio"

### 5 Concern Points

Concerns Suhyun identified while developing the concept:

**Concern 1: Lack of differentiation from broadcast programs**
After watching TVING's *Sync Game*, the realization: Zone X's vision is fundamentally similar to broadcast formats. If a broadcaster/OTT does this, they'll outcompete us on budget and casting.

**Concern 2: Lack of stakes — the core issue**
> "For both participants and viewers, this feels like an event tournament running as a broadcast program. The motivation for winning the competition isn't strong enough, and immersion breaks easily. You start watching and wonder: why am I watching this show's finale?"

This was conceptualized as the "absence of stakes":

| | Sync Game / Physical: 100 | MMA / Black Combat |
|---|---|---|
| Meaning of winning | Won this show | Real rankings and reputation change |
| Cost of losing | Eliminated from this show | Status in the community, future fight opportunities |
| Participant motivation | Appearance fee / exposure | Career, the honor of their discipline |
| Viewer investment | "Fun show" | "The fight of the athlete/sport I root for" |

→ **What Zone X must have: stakes that matter beyond the show.** This is the key fork between a "league" and a "show."

**Concern 3: Watchability of cardio format**
"Cardio movements are repetitive, and even watching the CrossFit Games gets boring." Trying to solve this through game design just brings you back to a broadcast format.

**Concern 4: Legitimacy of the competition**
Sehan's question from the kickoff — "Which sport develops human capability the most?" — is not just a naming choice. It's directly tied to "what game format should athletes compete in," which is a critically important question.

**Concern 5: Physical: 100 association problem**
Whether team-based or individual, it's hard to escape the "fitness survival" structure.

## 1. New Direction: An MMA-Style League

### The Core Pivot

**Street Woman Fighter (transplanting a broadcast format) → MMA/UFC (creating the league itself)**

Just as MMA built a new league out of the ecosystem of boxing, wrestling, jiu-jitsu, and other combat disciplines — Zone X creates one cardio league out of the broader lifestyle sports world: CrossFit, HYROX, running, MMA, etc.

> **"Just as MMA is the league that determines the most complete fighter, Zone X is the league that determines the most complete athlete."**

| | Original ("Transplanting SWF") | New Direction ("MMA-style League") |
|---|---|---|
| Protagonist | Team vs. team game | Individual athlete + sport background |
| Structure | Team survival tournament | 1v1 match → rankings → championship |
| Narrative source | In-game drama between/within teams | Athlete career, discipline honor, ranking battles |
| Format impression | Broadcast program | Sports league |
| Differentiation source | Game design | The league structure itself |

### Confirmed Decisions

**Unit of participation** — Individual athletes as the default unit. The process of sourcing participants through a "pro audition" tournament (like Black Combat's early days) is itself content.

**Season structure** — Season 1 = audition-style tournament → then the main league.

**Participant scope** — Not just CrossFit/HYROX, but also runners, MMA athletes, jiu-jitsu practitioners, recreational basketball/soccer league players.

**Match classes** — Like MMA weight classes: divided into 1v1 / 2v2 / 4v4 match formats.

## 2. Game Format Design: Phase System + Choose Your Attack

### Core Conditions Suhyun Set for the Design

1. **Simultaneous satisfaction of fun and legitimacy**: "Competition must happen in a short time, and there has to be enough of a standard to give the winner legitimate 'this person is better' credibility — not just a one-off event."
2. **Direct interaction**: "Direct interaction format seems like it would generate more entertainment value."
3. **Movement composition**: Machine (assault bike) + bodyweight (wall walks, rope climb, double-unders, burpees, box jumps). As long as it's not exclusively CrossFit-specific, it's fine.

### Convergence from 3 Approaches

Three approaches emerged in brainstorming:
1. Round dominance judging (transplanting combat sports structure)
2. Survival escalator (elimination-based)
3. Mutual interference battle (direct interaction)

Initial recommendation: Approach 2 as the base + partial introduction of Approach 3 elements.

### Suhyun's Pivotal Feedback (the moment that changed direction)

> "I know the CrossFit Open has high strategic completeness. It's important that this *doesn't* immediately evoke or invite direct comparison to CrossFit Open or HYROX. **It has to feel like Combat.** Mutual interaction is part of that, and Approach 3 elements need to be incorporated heavily. It needs back-and-forth tension and excitement."

This feedback elevated Approach 3 from "a small addition" to the **core mechanism**. The "lead advantage" system was derived from this.

### Suhyun's Additional Idea → Birth of Choose Your Attack

> "In MMA, fighters don't know their opponent's strategy — they predict and prepare. If all the WOD movements are fixed in advance, it can feel predictable and obvious. We could add an element where each Phase, athletes choose from a set of options."

→ "Lead Advantage" + "Element of Choice" = **Choose Your Attack** system.

### Confirmed Match Structure

```
Phase 1 — Fixed Signature (Opening Bell)
 └→ Milestone leader → earns "Choose Your Attack" selection
Phase 2 — Perform selected movements
 └→ Milestone leader → earns selection
Phase 3 — Perform selected movements
 └→ First to quit/fail = loss. If both complete, total time decides.
```

**Phase 1 — Signature Opening (always the same)**
- Example: Assault Bike 15 cal + 10 Burpees
- Zone X's "Opening Bell." A signature moment — like HYROX's 1km opening run.

**Milestone Leader → Choose Your Attack**
The athlete who completes a Phase first chooses the next Phase's movements from 2–3 options.

| Option | Example Movements | Character |
|---|---|---|
| "Upper Body Attack" | Rope Climb + Wall Walks | Lethal for runners/cyclists |
| "Cardio Attack" | Double-Unders + Extra Bike | A burden for strength-dominant athletes |
| "Lower Body Attack" | Box Jumps + Running | Relatively favorable for upper-body-dominant athletes |

### Why This Is Combat, Not a WOD

| CrossFit Open / HYROX | Zone X |
|---|---|
| Opponent: Timer/leaderboard (indirect) | Opponent: The person suffering right next to you (direct) |
| Pacing reference: My optimal strategy | Pacing reference: Adjusting strategy based on opponent's state |
| Source of tension: Race against time | Source of tension: Battle against the opponent |
| Failure: Not completing in time | Failure: Breaking down before your opponent does |

### 4 Core Mechanisms

1. **The faster I go, the harder it gets for my opponent** — Earning the milestone → choosing to attack the opponent's weakness
2. **But going too hard early drains my own energy first** — Pacing tradeoff
3. **The moment the opponent earns the milestone and attacks my weakness feels like a "hit"** — Combat texture
4. **Control can shift every Phase, automatically creating back-and-forth structure**

### Choose Your Attack: Alternative Options Evaluated

At Suhyun's request, 4 alternatives were ideated and compared:

| | Choose Your Attack (Original) | Pre-Match Draft | Blind Pick | Escalating Stakes | Rotating |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Combat feel | Strong | Medium | Strong | Strong | Medium |
| Legitimacy | Strong | Strongest | Medium | Medium | Strong |
| Intuitiveness | Strong | Strong | Weak | Medium | Strongest |
| Comeback potential | Strong | Medium | Medium | Strong | Medium |

**Conclusion**: Choose Your Attack as the base, with Escalating Stakes partially introduced for championship/title bouts.

## 3. Type Matchup System Design (In Progress)

### Design Principles

Derived from two key pieces of feedback from Suhyun:

1. **The need for type matchups**: "MMA has matchup dynamics between strikers and grapplers. In cardio, there are matchup dynamics between cardio-endurance types and strength-endurance types. Strategic elements are needed." But also: "If fitness ability alone determines everything, you get the criticism that it's just CrossFit Games/HYROX with a different format — and no different from Physical: 100."

2. **Pacing tradeoff**: "In cardio, the faster you go early, the more physical burden you carry late. If you push hard to dominate your opponent and drain your energy too fast, you can get reversed. That structure sounds entertaining."

From these, the design principle emerged:
> **"Keep the classification simple — exceptions are what create drama"**

Why MMA succeeded starting with just "striker vs. grappler" — audiences can understand the matchup within 5 minutes. A complex type classification system loses fan interest before they can memorize it.

### 3 Fitness Type Classifications

| Type | Nickname | Real Background | Core Strength | Core Weakness | Narrative |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cardio-Endurance** | The Grinder | Runners, HYROX, Triathletes | Sustained output, pacing, recovery | Explosive strength movements | "The one who never tires" |
| **Strength-Endurance** | The Engine | CrossFit, Weightlifting | Compound strength movements, upper body | Purely aerobic sustained intervals | "The one who breaks through with power" |
| **Explosive Power** | The Spark | MMA, Boxing, Recreational sport leagues | Short/intense intervals, total-body coordination | Gets worse the longer it goes | "The one who bets everything on the first round" |

### Type Matchup Matrix (Rock-Paper-Scissors Structure)

```
Cardio-Endurance (Grinder) → Favorable vs. Explosive Power (Spark)
  After the burst, Spark's energy tank runs out first in sustained intervals

Explosive Power (Spark) → Favorable vs. Strength-Endurance (Engine)
  Short, intense intervals break Engine's stable rhythm
  Strength-endurance types operate on "efficiency optimization" — when explosive athletes break the rhythm, efficiency collapses

Strength-Endurance (Engine) → Favorable vs. Cardio-Endurance (Grinder)
  In upper body/compound movement Phases, cardio types simply don't have the output
  Choose "Upper Body Attack" in Choose Your Attack → direct hit on Grinder
```

### Connection to Choose Your Attack

The type matchup system is what makes the Choose Your Attack selections into "strategy":

| Situation | Favorable Choice | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Engine matched against Grinder | Choose "Upper Body Attack" | Direct hit on cardio type's weakness → maximizes matchup advantage |
| Spark matched against Engine | All-in on Phase 1 → claim milestone first | Secure selection rights with own strength (explosive burst) |
| Grinder matched against Spark | Choose "Cardio Attack" | Dragging it into sustained intervals → Spark self-destructs |

### The Key: 4 Scenarios Where Exceptions Create Drama

Matchup types provide "expectation," and exceptions provide "story." Without matchups, there's no pre-match analysis. Without exceptions, there's no drama.

**① Overspec Exception — "This athlete transcended what their sport is supposed to produce"**
A Strength-Endurance type wins even the cardio interval. Overcomes an unfavorable matchup through sheer ability.
→ "I thought that athlete only did CrossFit — but their cardio is at that level?"

**② Strategic Reversal — "Won an unfavorable matchup with their head"**
Explosive type faces a cardio-endurance type — unfavorable matchup. But goes all-in on Phase 1, claims the milestone first, and selects "Upper Body Attack" → completely eliminates the cardio interval (their own weakness).
→ The moment Choose Your Attack is used to circumvent the matchup.

**③ Pacing Reversal — "Pushed too hard trying to win"**
Cardio-endurance type keeps choosing "Cardio Attack," using the favorable matchup — then in Phase 3, they collapse from overexertion before their opponent.
→ Zone X's unique kind of defeat. Even a favorable matchup can be reversed by a pacing mistake.

**④ Background Betrayal — "Sports are done by people, not disciplines"**
A recreational soccer player (Spark) beats a CrossFit athlete (Engine).
→ Demonstrates Zone X's "most complete athlete" narrative in action. Reinforces the league's identity.

## Core Principles (Applied to All Design Decisions)

1. **Zone X is a "league," not a "show."** Stakes that matter beyond the show.
2. **It's a battle, not a race.** Attack the opponent's weaknesses. Use pacing strategy to reverse.
3. **It must not invite immediate comparison to CrossFit Games/HYROX.** Combat texture is non-negotiable.
4. **Legitimacy and entertainment must be satisfied simultaneously.**
5. **Fitness ability alone isn't enough. Neither is strategy alone.** The balance of both is Zone X's unique territory.
6. **Make it signature.** Just as HYROX made the 1-hour race its own, Zone X must own its format.
