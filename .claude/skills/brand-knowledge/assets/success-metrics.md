## How to Use in Sales Conversations

### Setting Expectations

**Good:**
"Based on similar brands, you can expect [realistic range]. Here's what we've seen with [case study]."

**Bad:**
"We guarantee you'll get [specific number]."

### Demonstrating Value

**Good:**
"When we worked with [brand], we achieved [specific result]. That translated to [business outcome]."

**Bad:**
"We get amazing results for everyone."

### Handling "Prove it" Requests

**Good:**
"Let me show you specific numbers from [case study]. Here's what we achieved and the context... [walk through metrics]"

**Bad:**
"Trust us, we're really good."

### Competitive Comparison

**Good:**
"Industry average for [metric] is [%]. We achieved [%] with [client], which is [X]x better."

**Bad:**
"We're better than other agencies." (no proof)

## Important Notes on Using Metrics

**DO:**
✓ Provide context (industry, category, timeframe)
✓ Show realistic ranges, not single numbers
✓ Explain why metrics matter for their business
✓ Compare to benchmarks
✓ Update metrics as you gather more data

**DON'T:**
✗ Guarantee specific numbers
✗ Cherry-pick only best results
✗ Ignore context or category differences
✗ Make unfounded projections
✗ Use outdated or inaccurate data

---

**Remember:** These metrics are proof points, not promises. Use them to demonstrate capability and set realistic expectations, not to overpromise. Always provide context and explain what drives these numbers.

**Update Regularly:** As new case studies complete, update this document with real performance data. The more proof points, the stronger the sales materials.
