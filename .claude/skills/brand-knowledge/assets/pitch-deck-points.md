# Pitch Deck Points

Slide-by-slide structure for client pitches. Adapt based on audience and time available (10-min, 20-min, or 45-min versions).

---

## Standard Pitch Structure (20 minutes)

**Total slides: 12-15**
**Timing: 1-2 min per slide, leave 5-10 min for Q&A**

---

## SLIDE 1: Cover

**Visual:**
- Creatorhood logo
- Client logo
- Simple, clean background
- Meeting date

**Say:**
"Thank you for taking the time. Today I'll show you how we can [specific outcome for their business]."

**DON'T:**
- Overly designed or busy
- Multiple taglines
- Too much text

---

## SLIDE 2: Agenda

**Visual:**
- 3-4 bullet points max
- Simple list

**Content:**
1. Your Challenge (what we've observed)
2. Our Approach (how we solve it)
3. Proof It Works (case study)
4. What You Get (deliverables & investment)

**Say:**
"We'll cover four things in the next 15 minutes, then I'd love to hear your questions."

**Purpose:**
Set expectations, show respect for their time

---

## SLIDE 3: Who We Are (30 seconds)

**Visual:**
- Creatorhood logo
- One-line description
- 3-4 key credentials (numbers, clients, awards)

**Content:**
"Creatorhood is a media-first sports lifestyle company and content marketing agency.
- Founded [year]
- Clients: Kuksundang, Pelicana, Inhaze, [others]
- CH MAG: 1.6K sports lifestyle community
- Zone X: Original IP development"

**Say:**
"Quick intro: We're Creatorhood. We don't just make content—we build owned media engines. We've worked with brands like [most relevant to client]."

**DON'T:**
- Spend more than 30 seconds here
- Read your entire history
- Make it about you vs. them

---

## SLIDE 4: Your Challenge - Part 1 (Observation)

**Visual:**
- Screenshot of their current social OR
- Quote from discovery call OR
- Simple visual representing their challenge

**Content:**
"What we've observed about [Brand Name]'s current social presence:
- [Specific observation 1]
- [Specific observation 2]  
- [Specific observation 3]"

**Example for F&B brand:**
- Content feels advertising-heavy
- Younger demographics not engaging
- Beautiful posts but no storytelling

**Say:**
"When we looked at your Instagram, here's what we noticed... [walk through observations]"

**Purpose:**
Show you've done homework, understand their situation

**Critical:**
Be specific, not generic. Use THEIR actual content/situation.

---

## SLIDE 5: Your Challenge - Part 2 (Why It Matters)

**Visual:**
- Industry trend data OR
- Competitive comparison OR
- Audience behavior stats

**Content:**
"This matters because:
- [Industry context/trend]
- [Competitive pressure]
- [Audience expectation shift]"

**Example:**
- MZ generation skips obvious ads
- Competitors using documentary/authentic content
- Organic reach declining for promotional content

**Say:**
"This isn't just a [Brand Name] challenge. The whole [category] is facing [specific shift]. The brands winning are [what they're doing differently]."

**Purpose:**
Contextualize problem, show broader understanding

---

## SLIDE 6: Traditional Approach Fails

**Visual:**
- Two-column comparison OR
- Red X over old approach, green check for new

**Content:**
"Why Traditional Agency Approach Doesn't Work:
- [Problem 1 with old way]
- [Problem 2 with old way]
- [Problem 3 with old way]"

**Example (for e-commerce):**
Traditional Agency:
- Fixed fees regardless of results
- Pretty content, no sales connection
- Vanity metrics (likes, followers)

**Say:**
"Most agencies will propose [traditional approach]. Here's why that won't solve your challenge..."

**Purpose:**
Set up contrast for your approach, address elephant in room

**DON'T:**
- Bash competitors by name
- Be arrogant or dismissive
- Spend too long here (1 min max)

---

## SLIDE 7: Our Approach - Strategy

**Visual:**
- Simple diagram or framework
- Maximum 3-4 elements
- Clear, not complex

**Content:**
"[Strategic Framework Name]"

**For F&B (Scene-Based):**
"We show scenes where your product naturally exists:
1. Making Scene - [Brief description]
2. Consuming Scene - [Brief description]
3. Discovering Scene - [Brief description]"

**For E-commerce (Performance):**
"We create conversion-focused content with full attribution:
1. Conversion Content Types
2. Attribution System (UTM, GA4, coupons)
3. Revenue Share Model"

**For Heritage Brand (Repositioning):**
"We reframe your [X] years through retro lens:
1. Heritage Audit
2. Retro Visual System
3. Content Series"

**Say:**
"Our approach is [framework name]. Here's how it works... [explain each element simply]"

**Purpose:**
Show clear methodology, not random creativity

**Critical:**
ONE clear approach, not multiple options (confusing)

---

## SLIDE 8: What This Looks Like (Visuals)

**Visual:**
- Mock-ups of their content in your style OR
- Examples from similar case study
- 3-4 images/screenshots max

**Content:**
Show, don't just tell:
- What their feed would look like
- What a video would look like
- What the visual system would be

**Say:**
"Here's what this actually looks like... [walk through examples]"

**Purpose:**
Make it tangible and real, not abstract

**DON'T:**
- Show too many examples (overwhelming)
- Show work that's irrelevant to their category
- Make promises you can't deliver

---

## SLIDE 9: Case Study - The Challenge

**Visual:**
- Logo of case study brand
- Simple stats/context

**Content:**
"[Case Study Brand]:
- Industry: [Category]
- Challenge: [Similar to client]
- Timeframe: [Duration worked together]"

**Example (Kuksundang):**
"Kuksundang:
- Traditional Korean makgeolli brand
- Challenge: Limited MZ generation appeal
- Needed: Organic social growth without paid ads"

**Say:**
"Let me show you a brand we worked with that faced a similar challenge..."

**Purpose:**
Build credibility, show proof

**Critical:**
Choose case study most relevant to THIS client

---

## SLIDE 10: Case Study - Our Approach & Results

**Visual:**
- Before/after comparison OR
- Key content examples OR
- Results graph/numbers

**Content:**
"What We Did:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
- [Action 3]

Results:
- [Specific outcome 1]
- [Specific outcome 2]
- [Specific outcome 3]"

**Example (Kuksundang):**
"What We Did:
- Created 3 documentary series ('현장' concept)
- No paid advertising, organic only
- Cultural positioning vs. product focus

Results:
- Expanded to MZ demographic
- Organic reach and engagement up
- Brand association shifted to cultural/lifestyle"

**Say:**
"Here's what we did and what happened... [walk through outcomes]"

**Purpose:**
Prove methodology works, not just theory

**Critical:**
Real numbers/outcomes, not vague claims

---

## SLIDE 11: What You'll Get - Deliverables

**Visual:**
- Timeline graphic OR
- Checklist of deliverables OR
- Monthly calendar example

**Content:**
"What You'll Receive:

Month 1:
- [Specific deliverables]
- [Specific deliverables]

Ongoing (Monthly):
- [X] shortform videos
- [X] image posts
- Caption copywriting
- Performance reports
- Strategy sessions"

**Say:**
"Here's exactly what you'll get... [walk through concrete deliverables]"

**Purpose:**
Clarity on scope, no ambiguity

**DON'T:**
- Vague terms ("content creation")
- Overpromise quantity
- Hide what's NOT included

---

## SLIDE 12: Investment & Timeline

**Visual:**
- Clean pricing table OR
- Timeline with pricing OR
- Simple comparison of options (if multiple)

**Content:**
"Investment:
- Month 1 (Foundation): ₩[X]M
- Months 2+: ₩[Y]M per month
- [or] Revenue Share: [Z]% of attributed sales

Timeline:
- Week 1-2: Strategy & planning
- Week 3-4: First content production
- Week 5+: Launch & ongoing optimization"

**Say:**
"The investment for this program is [amount], which includes [what's included]. We can start [timeframe] and have first content live within [weeks]."

**Purpose:**
Transparency, remove uncertainty

**Critical:**
Be clear and direct, not apologetic about pricing

---

## SLIDE 13: Why Creatorhood

**Visual:**
- 3-4 key differentiators
- Simple icons or bullets
- Clean, confident

**Content:**
"Why Work With Us:
1. Proven Frameworks - [Brief description]
2. Business Outcomes - [Brief description]
3. Performance Models - [Brief description]
4. [Category] Expertise - [Brief description]"

**Example:**
1. Proven Frameworks - Scene-based strategy, not random posts
2. Business Outcomes - Focus on sales/transformation, not vanity metrics
3. Performance Models - Revenue share available, aligned incentives
4. F&B Expertise - Kuksundang, Pelicana, category understanding

**Say:**
"Why us vs. other agencies? Four reasons... [walk through briefly]"

**Purpose:**
Differentiate, competitive positioning

**DON'T:**
- List 10 reasons (dilutes message)
- Bash competitors
- Be arrogant

---

## SLIDE 14: Next Steps

**Visual:**
- 3-4 simple steps
- Numbers or timeline
- Clear path forward

**Content:**
"Next Steps:
1. Q&A and discussion (today)
2. Internal review and questions (this week)
3. Agreement and kickoff (next week)
4. First content live (3-4 weeks from start)"

**Say:**
"If this makes sense, here's how we move forward... [walk through steps, make it easy]"

**Purpose:**
Clear call to action, remove friction

**Critical:**
Make it easy to say yes, low pressure

---

## SLIDE 15: Thank You / Q&A

**Visual:**
- Simple "Questions?" or "Thank You"
- Contact information
- Creatorhood logo

**Content:**
[Your Name]
[Email]
[Phone]

**Say:**
"That's what I wanted to share. What questions do you have?"

**Then:**
STOP TALKING. Let them process and ask questions.

## Timing Adaptations

### 10-Minute Version (Quick Pitch)

**Keep only:**
1. Cover
2. Who We Are (15 sec)
3. Your Challenge (1 min)
4. Our Approach (2 min)
5. Case Study (2 min - combined challenge/results)
6. What You Get (1 min)
7. Investment (1 min)
8. Next Steps (30 sec)
9. Q&A (2+ min)

### 45-Minute Version (Deep Dive)

**Add:**
- Detailed case study (multiple slides)
- Content examples (more visuals)
- Team introduction
- Detailed deliverables breakdown
- FAQ slides
- Appendix with additional proof

## Pitch Delivery Tips

### Before Pitch

**Prepare:**
□ Research client thoroughly
□ Customize all slides (remove generic content)
□ Practice timing (stay under limit)
□ Prepare for common objections
□ Have backup materials ready

**Technical:**
□ Test screen share if remote
□ Have PDF backup if PowerPoint fails
□ Send deck in advance if requested (or not - preference varies)

### During Pitch

**Opening (First 30 seconds):**
- Thank them for time
- Brief intro (name, role)
- Set agenda
- Ask about their time constraint

**Middle (Content):**
- Speak TO them, not AT them
- Make eye contact (or camera)
- Pause for questions
- Read the room (speed up/slow down)
- Connect content to THEIR situation

**Handling Questions:**
- Don't interrupt
- Repeat question (shows you heard)
- Answer directly, don't dodge
- If you don't know, say so ("Let me find out and get back to you")

**Closing:**
- Summarize key points (30 sec)
- Clear next steps
- Thank them again
- Ask for the business (if appropriate)

### After Pitch

**Immediate:**
- Send thank you email (within 2 hours)
- Attach deck if not already sent
- Summarize next steps agreed upon

**Follow-Up:**
- Check in within 3-5 days if no response
- Provide additional information if requested
- Be patient but persistent

## Common Objections & Responses

### "Your price is too high"

**Response:**
"I understand budget is a consideration. Can I ask what you're comparing to? [Listen] The way I'd think about this is as an investment, not a cost. If we achieve [specific outcome], what would that be worth to your business? We also have [flexible options] if the standard program doesn't fit your current budget."

### "We need to think about it"

**Response:**
"Of course, this is an important decision. Can I ask what specific areas you need to think through? [Listen] I'm happy to provide more information on those points."

**Then address their specific concerns.**

### "Can you just execute our strategy?"

**Response:**
"We can absolutely work within parameters you set. However, our real value is in the strategic thinking—that's where we've seen the biggest impact for clients like [case study]. Would you be open to a strategy workshop first, then decide if our approach makes sense?"

### "We've tried agencies before, it didn't work"

**Response:**
"I appreciate you sharing that. What specifically didn't work? [Listen carefully] That's actually exactly why we approach things differently. [Explain your difference based on their specific pain point]"

### "We need results faster"

**Response:**
"Faster than what timeframe? [Get specific] Let's be realistic about what's achievable. For [metric they care about], we typically see [timeframe]. We can set monthly milestones so you can see progress along the way. Does that work?"

### "How do we know you'll deliver?"

**Response:**
"Great question. Here's how we de-risk it: [Pilot period / performance model / milestone payments / testimonials]. We have every incentive to deliver because [explain]. What would make you feel comfortable?"

## Pitch Deck Variations by Client Type

### For F&B / Traditional Brand

**Emphasize:**
- Documentary / authentic approach
- Heritage repositioning (if applicable)
- Organic growth (not paid ad dependent)
- Cultural positioning

**Case Studies:**
- Kuksundang (documentary)
- Pelicana (heritage)

### For E-commerce

**Emphasize:**
- Performance partnership model
- Attribution and tracking
- Conversion-focused content
- ROI and ROAS

**Case Study:**
- Inhaze (revenue share)

### For Startup / Emerging Brand

**Emphasize:**
- Flexibility and scalability
- Performance options (reduce risk)
- Fast execution
- Building from zero

**Case Study:**
- CH MAG (built from scratch)
- Inhaze (flexible model)

### For Corporate / Enterprise

**Emphasize:**
- Process and professionalism
- Proven frameworks
- Reporting and accountability
- Team credentials

**Case Study:**
- Most established brand client
- Detailed results and metrics

## Visual Design Tips

**Do:**
✓ Clean, minimal design
✓ High-quality images only
✓ Consistent fonts and colors
✓ Plenty of white space
✓ One main idea per slide
✓ Use client's brand colors subtly

**Don't:**
✗ Stock photos of handshakes/business people
✗ Cheesy graphics or clip art
✗ Too much text (if you need full text, it's a doc not a deck)
✗ Animation (unless very subtle)
✗ Multiple fonts
✗ Bright, garish colors

---

## Final Checklist Before Pitch

□ Deck customized for THIS client (no generic "Company X")
□ Case study relevant to their situation
□ Numbers/outcomes are accurate and up-to-date
□ Pricing is current and clear
□ Contact info is correct
□ All links work (if included)
□ Deck is saved in compatible format
□ Practiced timing (under limit with buffer)
□ Prepared for common objections
□ Know your next steps if they say yes

---

**Remember:** The deck supports your narrative. YOU are the pitch, not the slides. Make eye contact, read the room, and have a conversation—don't just read slides.

**Most important slide:** Slide 4 (Their Challenge). If you nail understanding their problem, the rest flows naturally. If you miss this, nothing else matters.
