## Case Study 1: Kuksundang (국순당)

### Client Profile
- **Company**: Kuksundang, Korea's leading traditional alcohol company
- **Product**: 국순당생막걸리 (traditional Korean makgeolli)
- **Industry**: F&B / Alcoholic Beverages
- **Challenge**: Traditional brand struggling to connect with MZ generation

### The Challenge

**Business Problem:**
- Makgeolli perceived as "old people's drink"
- Limited appeal to 20-30s demographic
- Competing against trendy craft beverages
- Low social media engagement despite brand recognition

**Client Need:**
- Expand to younger demographics without alienating existing base
- Build authentic social presence
- Drive brand relevance in contemporary culture
- Achieve results without heavy paid advertising budget

### Our Solution: "막걸리 현장" (The Makgeolli Scene) Strategy

**Strategic Insight:**
Client wanted to communicate "검색해도 나오지 않는 이야기가 막걸리에 있다" (There are stories about makgeolli that you can't find by searching).

We abstracted this into **"현장" (scene)** concept:
- Search = static data, past information
- Scene = living stories happening now
- Show moments where makgeolli naturally exists

**Key Decision:**
DON'T say "막걸리에는 이런 이야기가 있어요" (typical advertising)  
DO create documentary content that organically reveals these stories

### What We Delivered

**Three Documentary Content Series:**

**1. 순당포착 (Sundang Caught) - The Making Scene**
- Format: Behind-the-scenes documentary shorts
- Content: Brewing process, makers' stories, seasonal variations
- Tone: Observational, minimal narration, authentic
- Example: "Why this brewery only uses certain nuruk in winter"

**2. Mic Drop - The Drinking Scene**
- Format: Conversation-driven content
- Content: People talking over makgeolli, space atmosphere
- Tone: Intimate, genuine dialogue, comfortable silences
- Example: "Late-night pojangmacha conversations"

**3. 외국인이 막걸리를 가져왔다 - The Discovery Scene**
- Format: Outsider perspective
- Content: Foreigners experiencing makgeolli culture
- Tone: Curious, cross-cultural, educational through discovery
- Example: "French sommelier tries traditional makgeolli"

**Integrated Campaign: Makgeolli x Taco Week (Dec 2024)**
- Collaboration with Pescaderia (타코 restaurant)
- Concept: "도깨비 크리스마스" (Dokkaebi Christmas)
- Makgeolli cocktails paired with tacos
- Influencer recruitment and on-site content capture

**Innovation: AI-Generated Advertising**
- Used Higgsfield, Kling, Veo, Nano Banana for creative ads
- Surreal concepts impossible with traditional photography
- Cost-effective test campaigns
- Examples: Paper craft universe, neon hanok, fermentation cosmos

### Results & Impact

**Organic Growth (No Paid Advertising):**
- Social media engagement increased significantly
- Younger demographic (20-30s) follower growth
- Content shared beyond immediate followers
- User-generated content incorporating brand

**Brand Perception Shift:**
- From "traditional alcohol" to "cultural experience"
- MZ generation commenting and engaging
- Makgeolli associated with "현장" and authentic moments
- Media coverage of campaigns

**Business Impact:**
- Increased brand searches
- Younger customers in retail data
- Successful collaborative events
- Content library for long-term use

### Key Learnings (Reusable)

**1. Abstract Brand Messages**
Direct advertising → Concept that people relate to  
"Buy our makgeolli" → "Moments where makgeolli naturally exists"

**2. Documentary > Advertising**
People don't want to be sold to  
They want authentic stories they can discover

**3. Series Build Audiences**
One-off content = views  
Series format = recurring viewership and anticipation

**4. Heritage Can Be Contemporary**
Traditional doesn't mean old-fashioned  
Reframe heritage through modern lens (scenes, moments, culture)

### Applicable To

✅ **Perfect for:**
- F&B brands with heritage
- Traditional products targeting younger demos
- Brands wanting authentic (not advertising) feel
- Clients with limited paid ad budgets

✅ **Strategic elements to reuse:**
- Scene-based content strategy
- Three-series documentary model
- Heritage repositioning approach
- Organic social growth tactics

## Case Study 2: Inhaze (인헤이즈)

### Client Profile
- **Company**: Inhaze (skincare/wellness e-commerce brand)
- **Category**: E-commerce / Beauty & Wellness
- **Business Model**: Direct-to-consumer online sales
- **Challenge**: Content not driving measurable sales

### The Challenge

**Business Problem:**
- High-volume "magazine-style" content not converting to sales
- Difficult to justify ongoing content investment
- Can't prove ROI of organic social content
- Content looked good but didn't drive business

**Client Need:**
- Direct connection between content and sales
- Performance-based partnership (reduce financial risk)
- Measurable attribution system
- Proof that organic content can drive revenue

### Our Solution: Revenue Share Performance Model

**Strategic Pivot:**
FROM: Brand-as-Magazine (aesthetics-first, high volume)  
TO: Performance Partnership (conversion-first, attribution-based)

**Core Principle:**
"We only win when you win" - Revenue share aligns our incentives with client success

### What We Delivered

**New Content Strategy: Conversion-Focused**

**Content Types Optimized for Sales:**

1. **Problem → Solution Format**
   - Hook: Identify customer pain point
   - Show product as solution
   - Explain why it works
   - Clear CTA with tracking

2. **Before/After Transformation**
   - Visual proof of results
   - Realistic timeframes
   - Real users, not models
   - Measurable changes shown

3. **Tutorial/How-To Content**
   - Step-by-step product usage
   - Tips for best results
   - Common mistakes to avoid
   - Makes purchase less intimidating

4. **Social Proof/Reviews**
   - User testimonials
   - Unboxing reactions
   - "I was skeptical but..." format
   - Build trust through others

5. **Product Education**
   - Ingredient breakdowns
   - "Why I switched" narratives
   - Price-to-value comparisons
   - Informed purchase decisions

**Attribution System:**
- Unique UTM parameters for all content links
- Dedicated coupon codes per campaign
- Google Analytics 4 e-commerce tracking
- Link-in-bio conversion tracking
- Monthly sales reconciliation

**Revenue Share Structure:**
- Option A: 0% base fee, 15-20% of attributed sales (high risk/reward)
- Option B: Small base fee (₩500K-1M), 10-15% of attributed sales (balanced)
- Option C: Tiered % based on sales volume (incentivizes growth)
- 3-month pilot to prove model

### Results & Impact

**Performance Metrics (Target After 3 Months):**
- Monthly attributed sales: ₩10-15M
- Our revenue: ₩1.5M-3M (vs. fixed fee model)
- CAC reduction: 35% vs. paid channels
- ROAS: 3.5-4.0 (₩3.50-4 revenue per ₩1 content investment)
- Conversion rate: 2%+ from our traffic

**Process Transformation:**
- Weekly performance snapshots
- Bi-weekly strategy sessions with client
- Monthly comprehensive reports
- Data-driven content iteration
- Quick pivots based on what converts

**Client Benefit:**
- Pay only for results
- Reduced financial risk
- Clear ROI visibility
- Aligned partnership incentives
- Continuous optimization

### Key Learnings (Reusable)

**1. Magazine Model Has Limits**
Pretty content builds brand but not necessarily sales  
Commerce brands need conversion-focused strategy

**2. Attribution Proves Value**
Can't improve what you can't measure  
UTM + GA4 + coupon codes show clear impact

**3. Revenue Share Aligns Incentives**
When we win only if client wins, we focus on what matters  
Better than fixed fees that reward activity over results

**4. Content Types That Convert**
Before/after, tutorials, social proof > lifestyle aesthetics  
Direct value communication > abstract brand building

**5. Data-Driven Iteration**
Weekly reviews → identify winners → double down  
Monthly analysis → kill losers → test new approaches

### Applicable To

✅ **Perfect for:**
- E-commerce brands (fashion, beauty, food, lifestyle)
- Products with ₩30K-200K price points
- Brands with existing traffic (we amplify, not build from zero)
- Clients wanting performance-based partnerships

✅ **Strategic elements to reuse:**
- Revenue share partnership model
- Attribution methodology (UTM, coupons, GA4)
- Conversion content frameworks
- Performance reporting structure

⚠️ **Not suitable for:**
- B2B brands (long sales cycles)
- Offline-only businesses (attribution difficult)
- Luxury high-ticket items (very long consideration)
- Brands with very low margins

## Case Study 3: Pelicana (페리카나)

### Client Profile
- **Company**: Pelicana, Korean fried chicken chain
- **Heritage**: 26 years in business (est. 1998)
- **Industry**: F&B / Quick Service Restaurant
- **Challenge**: Perceived as "old brand" vs. trendy competitors

### The Challenge

**Business Problem:**
- 26-year legacy viewed as weakness, not strength
- Younger demographics choosing newer, trendier chicken brands
- Generic F&B social media approach
- SNS felt like advertising channel, not content destination
- Competing in crowded fried chicken market

**Client Need:**
- Reposition heritage as competitive advantage
- Attract MZ generation without alienating existing customers
- Stand out in saturated social media landscape
- Transform social presence from ads to content

### Our Solution: "Heritage Through Retro" Strategy

**Strategic Insight:**
Don't hide 26 years—celebrate it through retro lens  
"26년 유산을 레트로로 재해석" (Reinterpret 26-year heritage through retro)

**Key Positioning Shift:**
FROM: "We're an old chicken brand"  
TO: "We're THE chicken brand from Korea's coolest eras"

**Why Retro Works:**
- MZ generation nostalgic for 90s-2000s they didn't fully experience
- Y2K aesthetic having major resurgence
- Pelicana launched 1998 (peak Y2K) - can authentically claim this
- Newer brands can't replicate genuine heritage

### What We Delivered

**Retro Visual System:**

**[Midiigital] Design Language**
- 90s-early 2000s digital era aesthetic
- Y2K futurism meets analog warmth
- Chunky typography, pixel fonts, pastels
- Collage layouts, film grain, halftone textures
- Refined execution (not kitschy)

**[Video] Film Look Cinematics**
- 16mm/Super 8 film emulation
- Handheld but intentional camera work
- Natural lighting, warm color grading
- Longer takes (not TikTok pace)
- Vintage sound design with vinyl crackle

**Content Series Structure (Proposed):**

**Shortform Videos (4-5/month):**

1. **"페리카나 순간" (Pelicana Moments)**
   - 30-60 sec cinematic short vlogs
   - Meaningful moments where Pelicana naturally exists
   - Examples: Family celebration, late-night study snack, friend reconciliation
   - Film look, nostalgic grading, minimal dialogue

2. **"26년 아카이브" (26-Year Archive)**
   - 30-45 sec heritage content
   - Remastered archival footage + recreations
   - Examples: First store 1998, memorable CFs, three generations
   - VHS aesthetic, nostalgic music

3. **"페리카나X" (Pelicana Collaborations)**
   - 30-60 sec creator partnerships
   - Examples: Musician backstage, athlete post-game, chef reimagining
   - Flexible format matching collaborator style

**Image Posts (10-11/month):**

1. **Retro Editorial (2-3/month)**
   - High-concept editorial photography
   - 90s props, film photography look, retro typography
   - Portfolio-quality aesthetic value

2. **Trend Memes/Viral (2-3/month)**
   - Current trends with Pelicana twist
   - Text-heavy, platform-native, humorous
   - High engagement potential

3. **Information Posts (1-2/month)**
   - Menu, promotions, locations
   - Retro-styled but clear information
   - Minimal to avoid "ad channel" feel

**Monthly Output:** 15 total pieces (balanced content mix)

**Strategic Positioning:**
- FROM: Advertising channel
- TO: Content studio (people follow for content, not just coupons)

### Results & Impact (Projected Goals)

**Engagement Metrics (3-6 months):**
- Instagram engagement rate: 2x current baseline
- Reels average views: 50K+ per video
- Follower growth: +10% monthly
- Save rate increase (indicates content value)

**Brand Perception:**
- Comments shift: "old brand" → "cool brand"
- User-generated content using retro theme
- Media mentions linking Pelicana to retro trend
- "Pelicana = retro chicken" keyword association

**Business Impact:**
- Foot traffic attribution (where trackable)
- Coupon redemption from social
- Brand search volume trending up
- Younger demographic in customer data

### Key Learnings (Reusable)

**1. Heritage Is Strength When Reframed**
Don't apologize for longevity  
Find contemporary lens that makes heritage cool (retro, documentary, cultural)

**2. Aesthetic Consistency Builds Recognition**
Retro visual system = instantly recognizable as Pelicana  
Consistency > one-off viral attempts

**3. Content Studio > Ad Channel**
People follow brands that create content they want  
Not brands that just promote products

**4. Nostalgia Bridges Generations**
Retro appeals to both MZ (cool) and 30-40s (authentic nostalgia)  
Single strategy, dual audience

**5. Film Quality Stands Out**
Investment in cinematic quality differentiates in crowded social space  
"Content studio" positioning requires content-level production

### Applicable To

✅ **Perfect for:**
- Legacy brands (15-30 years old)
- Korean brands from 90s-2000s era
- F&B or lifestyle brands with heritage
- Brands competing against trendy newcomers
- Clients wanting distinctive visual identity

✅ **Strategic elements to reuse:**
- Retro repositioning playbook
- Film look visual system
- 90s-Y2K aesthetic guidelines
- Content studio transformation approach
- Moment-based storytelling (not product-focused)

✅ **Adaptation for other eras:**
- 80s heritage → synthwave/neon aesthetic
- 70s heritage → vintage analog warmth
- 2000s heritage → early digital nostalgia

## Cross-Case Learnings

### Strategic Patterns That Work

**1. Abstract Brand Messages**
- Kuksundang: "막걸리 이야기" → "현장" (scenes)
- Pelicana: "26 years old" → "retro cool"
- Lesson: Make brand message relatable, not corporate

**2. Series > One-Offs**
- All three cases use series structure
- Recurring viewership, audience anticipation
- Brand as content destination

**3. Authenticity > Polish**
- Documentary approach (Kuksundang)
- Film look imperfection (Pelicana)
- Real customer stories (Inhaze)
- Lesson: Genuine beats overly produced

**4. Align Incentives**
- Revenue share model (Inhaze)
- Organic growth metrics (Kuksundang)
- Business-tied KPIs (Pelicana)
- Lesson: Our success depends on client success

**5. Heritage Can Be Contemporary**
- Kuksundang: Traditional → cultural
- Pelicana: 26 years → retro
- Lesson: Reframe, don't hide, legacy

### What Makes Our Approach Different

**Traditional Agency Approach:**
- Execute client brief as-is
- Optimize for engagement/reach
- Fixed deliverables regardless of results
- Campaign mentality (start/stop)

**Creatorhood Approach:**
- Challenge brief, propose better strategy
- Optimize for business outcomes
- Performance-based or aligned incentives
- Long-term content engine building

### How to Use These Cases

**In Client Pitches:**
- Match prospect to similar case (category, challenge, goals)
- Show strategic thinking, not just execution
- Emphasize outcomes and learnings
- Adapt frameworks to their context

**In Strategy Development:**

- Don't copy, adapt to new brand
