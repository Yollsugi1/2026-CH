# Pricing Strategy

How and why we price the way we do. Our pricing philosophy, methodology, and strategic considerations.

---

## Core Pricing Philosophy

### 1. Value-Based, Not Cost-Plus

**Traditional Agency Approach:**
```
Our costs + Desired margin = Price
```
- Focus on internal costs
- Margin percentage targets
- Race to the bottom on competition

**Creatorhood Approach:**
```
Client's value received + Market positioning = Price
```
- Focus on value delivered
- What outcomes are worth to client
- Premium positioning for premium work

**In Practice:**
- Small client getting ₩10M value → Charge ₩2-3M (20-30% of value)
- Large client getting ₩100M value → Charge ₩5-8M (5-8% of value, but higher absolute)
- Strategic work (repositioning) priced higher than execution work
- Performance models align value directly (we win when they win)

###2. Sustainable Economics

**We Need to Make Money:**
- Cover team salaries and overhead
- Invest in tools and capabilities
- Build for long-term sustainability
- Attract and retain talent

**Target Margins:**
- Strategy work: 60-70% gross margin (high expertise, low hard costs)
- Content production: 40-50% gross margin (equipment, production costs)
- Full-service retainers: 45-55% gross margin (blended)
- Performance partnerships: Variable (higher risk, higher potential upside)

**Minimum Viable Pricing:**
- Below ₩1.5M/month rarely sustainable
- Covers 1-2 people's time at most
- Can't deliver real value at rock-bottom prices
- Better to walk away than undercharge

### 3. Transparent, Not Hidden

**What Clients Appreciate:**
- Clear breakdown of what's included
- Explicit about exclusions
- No surprise fees
- Honest about pricing rationale

**What We Avoid:**
- Lowball initial quote, nickel-and-dime later
- Unclear scope that allows us to under-deliver
- "We'll figure it out as we go"
- Comparing only on price (vs. value)

### 4. Flexible for Alignment

**Different Clients, Different Models:**
- Established brands → Fixed retainers
- E-commerce → Performance models
- Startups → Starter packages, performance options
- Enterprise → Premium pricing, comprehensive scope
- Non-profits → Reduced rates (but still profitable)

**Flexibility is Strategy:**
- Shows we understand their business
- Creates lower barrier to entry (performance models)
- Allows for growth (starter → full service)
- Builds long-term relationships

## Pricing Methodology

### Step 1: Understand Value to Client

**Questions to Ask:**
- What business problem are they solving?
- What's the cost of NOT doing this?
- What's the potential upside if successful?
- What's their budget reality?
- What are competitive alternatives?

**Example - E-commerce Brand:**
- Problem: CAC too high (₩50K per customer)
- Current spend: ₩10M/month on paid ads
- If we reduce CAC to ₩30K: Save ₩4M/month
- Value to them: ₩4M/month ongoing
- Our price: ₩2M hybrid model (base + performance) = Great deal for them

**Example - Heritage Brand:**
- Problem: Losing younger customers
- Cost of problem: Declining revenue trajectory
- Value of solution: Repositioning opens new segment
- Value to them: Hard to quantify, but strategic importance high
- Our price: ₩4-5M/month for transformation = Justified investment

### Step 2: Calculate Base Cost

**Internal Cost Calculation:**
```
Team Time Required
├─ Strategist: X hours/month
├─ Producer: Y hours/month
├─ Designer: Z hours/month
└─ Manager: A hours/month

Total hours × Hourly rate = Base cost

Overhead multiplier (1.5-2x for agency)
= Internal cost
```

**Example - ₩3.5M Retainer:**
```
Strategist: 20 hours × ₩100K = ₩2M
Producer: 40 hours × ₩80K = ₩3.2M
Designer: 20 hours × ₩70K = ₩1.4M
Manager: 10 hours × ₩80K = ₩800K
---
Total: ₩7.4M in team time

Overhead & profit target: 50% margin
Minimum price: ₩3.7M

Market positioning: ₩3.5-4M range
Final price: ₩3.5M (acceptable margin, competitive)
```

### Step 3: Apply Market Context

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Traditional agencies: ₩5-8M for similar scope
- Freelancers: ₩1.5-3M (but inconsistent quality)
- In-house hire: ₩4-5M/month (salary + benefits + time to ramp)

**Our Position:**
- Premium vs. freelancers (proven frameworks, team capability)
- Competitive vs. traditional agencies (more value, less overhead)
- Strategic vs. execution shops (not just "do what you say")

**Pricing Sweet Spot:**
- High enough to be taken seriously
- Low enough to be accessible
- Justified by value and proof points

### Step 4: Structure Options

**Always Provide 2-3 Options:**

**Option A: Premium/Recommended**
- Everything they need and then some
- ₩[Higher end of range]
- "If you want the full transformation"

**Option B: Core/Standard**
- Essential services, great value
- ₩[Middle of range]
- "Most clients choose this"

**Option C: Alternative**
- Could be: Starter package, performance model, or project-based
- ₩[Lower or different structure]
- "If budget is tight or you want to test"

**Psychology:**
- Option A anchors high
- Option B seems reasonable by comparison
- Option C shows flexibility
- Most clients choose B (as intended)

## Pricing by Client Type

### Startups / Early-Stage

**Reality:**
- Limited budgets (₩1-3M/month typically)
- High risk of failure/pivot
- Need results fast
- Will grow if successful

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Starter packages (₩1.5-2.5M)
- Performance models when applicable (align incentives)
- 3-month pilots (low commitment)
- Upgrade path clearly defined

**Why It Works:**
- Lower barrier to entry
- We bet on their success
- If they scale, we scale with them
- Portfolio value if they become big

### Small-Medium Businesses (SMB)

**Reality:**
- Established but not huge budgets (₩2-5M/month)
- Need proven ROI
- Often owner-led decision making (fast decisions)
- Value scrappiness and results

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Growth bundles (₩3-4M)
- Clear deliverables and outcomes
- Flexible scope adjustments
- Performance bonuses when applicable

**Why It Works:**
- Sweet spot for our capabilities
- Reasonable budgets with realistic expectations
- Long-term relationship potential
- Can do great work without bureaucracy

### Enterprise / Large Brands

**Reality:**
- Bigger budgets available (₩5-10M+/month)
- Longer sales cycles
- More stakeholders
- Higher expectations and standards

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Premium positioning (₩5-8M+)
- Comprehensive scope
- Dedicated team mentions
- Account management and reporting rigor

**Why It Works:**
- Can afford premium pricing
- Appreciate proven frameworks and professionalism
- Want low-risk, high-quality partners
- Less price-sensitive, more value-focused

### Non-Profits / Social Impact

**Reality:**
- Very limited budgets
- Good cause appeal
- Potential for PR/portfolio value
- But still need to pay bills

**Pricing Strategy:**
- 20-30% discount from standard rates
- Still maintain minimum viability (₩1.5M+)
- Pro-bono very rarely (only if strategic value)
- Clear scope boundaries

**Why It Works:**
- Feel good factor
- Portfolio and PR value
- Still sustainable for us
- Help causes we care about

## Special Pricing Situations

### Friends & Family Discount

**The Temptation:**
"Can you help my friend's business? They don't have much budget..."

**The Problem:**
- Undervaluing work damages perceived value
- Can create awkward relationships if issues arise
- Sets bad precedent
- Often the most demanding clients

**Our Policy:**
- 10-15% "network rate" discount
- Not free (devalues work and relationship)
- Written agreement still required
- Clear that it's special consideration, not standard

**What to Say:**
"I'd love to help. My standard rate for this would be ₩[X], but for [connection], I can offer ₩[Y]. Does that work?"

### "Test Project" Requests

**The Ask:**
"Can you do a small test project first? We're not ready to commit."

**The Problem:**
- Often disguised as risk reduction, actually price shopping
- Real intent is to get cheap/free work
- Rarely converts to full engagement
- Wastes time and goodwill

**Our Policy:**
- Paid test projects only
- Scope must be meaningful (not just "one post")
- Price at normal rates (no discount for "test")
- Clear path to full engagement if successful

**What to Say:**
"I understand you want to test the partnership. I can offer a 3-month pilot at normal rates with easy exit after month 2 if it's not working. That gives us both real data without the complexity of a tiny test project. Sound fair?"

### Equity Instead of Cash

**The Ask:**
"We can't afford your rates but can offer equity..."

**The Reality:**
- Most startups fail (equity worth zero)
- Equity is future potential, cash is present reality
- Creates complex relationship dynamics
- Only works if we're true believers

**Our Policy:**
- Almost never accept equity-only
- Hybrid possible: Reduced cash + meaningful equity (5-10%)
- Must genuinely believe in company and team
- Legal agreement required (vesting, terms clear)

**What to Say:**
"I appreciate the offer. Equity can be interesting, but we need cash flow to operate. If you can do ₩[reduced amount] cash plus [meaningful equity %], we can explore that. Otherwise, let's revisit when you've raised funding."

### Rush / Urgent Projects

**The Situation:**
"We need this ASAP! Can you turn it around in [unreasonable timeframe]?"

**The Reality:**
- Rush projects disrupt workflow
- Lower quality due to compressed timeline
- Higher stress for team
- Often client's poor planning

**Our Policy:**
- Rush fee: +30-50% on standard pricing
- Only if truly urgent (not artificial urgency)
- Must be feasible (some things can't be rushed)
- Clear on what's possible in timeframe

**What to Say:**
"We can accommodate rush timelines with a 40% expedite fee. That covers rearranging our schedule and weekend work. Alternative: If we start [normal timeline], we can deliver higher quality work at standard pricing."

### Competitive Bidding / RFPs

**The Situation:**
"We're getting proposals from 3-4 agencies. What's your best price?"

**The Problem:**
- Often race to bottom on price
- Detailed proposals for 25% win rate
- Client isn't really comparing apples-to-apples
- Value differentiation gets lost

**Our Approach:**
- Qualify hard (do they value what we do?)
- Emphasize value, not lowest price
- Offer initial consultation (paid or unpaid depending on opportunity)
- Detailed proposal only if real fit

**What to Say:**
"I'd love to compete for your business, but not only on price. Can we have a conversation about what success looks like and how our approach is different? Then I'll provide a proposal that's competitive on value, not just cost."

## Performance-Based Pricing

### When to Use

**Good Fit:**
- ✅ E-commerce with clear attribution
- ✅ Direct-to-consumer sales
- ✅ Short sales cycle (hours to days)
- ✅ Products ₩30K-200K range
- ✅ Existing traffic (we amplify)

**Poor Fit:**
- ❌ B2B with long sales cycles
- ❌ Offline-only businesses (no tracking)
- ❌ Very high-ticket items (many touchpoints)
- ❌ Low margins (can't afford 15-20% share)
- ❌ Zero existing traffic (we'd be building from scratch)

### Structuring Performance Deals

**Pure Performance (High Risk/Reward):**
```
Base: ₩0
Share: 15-20% of attributed sales

Example:
₩10M monthly sales → We earn ₩1.5-2M
₩20M monthly sales → We earn ₩3-4M
₩0 sales → We earn ₩0 (our risk)
```

**Hybrid (Balanced):**
```
Base: ₩500K-1M (covers minimum costs)
Share: 10-15% of attributed sales

Example:
₩8M monthly sales → ₩800K base + ₩1.2M share = ₩2M total
₩15M monthly sales → ₩800K base + ₩2.25M share = ₩3.05M total
```

**Tiered (Incentivizes Growth):**
```
0-₩5M sales: 20% share
₩5-10M sales: 15% share
₩10M+ sales: 12% share

Example:
₩12M monthly sales:
- First ₩5M: ₩1M (20%)
- Next ₩5M: ₩750K (15%)
- Last ₩2M: ₩240K (12%)
- Total: ₩1.99M
```

### Attribution Methodology

**Must Be Transparent:**
- UTM parameters on all links
- Dedicated coupon codes
- GA4 e-commerce tracking
- Monthly reconciliation reports
- Client has visibility into tracking

**Attribution Models:**
- Last-click: Content that drove final purchase (conservative for us)
- First-click: Content that started journey (optimistic for us)
- Multi-touch: Weighted across touchpoints (most fair)

**Our Standard: Last-Click Attribution**
- Most conservative
- Easiest to verify
- Underestimates our impact (but builds trust)
- Can demonstrate additional influence separately

## Discounting Strategy

### When to Discount

**Good Reasons:**
- Volume commitment (6-12 month contract)
- Multiple services bundled
- Startup/early-stage with growth potential
- Strategic portfolio value
- Non-profit with mission alignment
- Network referral (modest 10-15%)

**Bad Reasons:**
- Just to win business (devalues work)
- Client claims "tight budget" (everyone does)
- Competitive pressure alone (race to bottom)
- "Exposure" promises (rarely materialize)
- Fear of losing deal (desperation pricing)

### Discount Levels

**Standard Discounts:**
- 3-month commit: 5%
- 6-month commit: 10%
- 12-month commit: 15%
- Bundle (3+ services): 10-15%
- Non-profit: 20-30%
- Network rate: 10-15%

**Maximum Discount:**
- Never go below 25% off standard rates
- Below that, we're not sustainable
- Better to walk away than undervalue work

### Discount Communication

**DON'T:**
"Okay, I can go down to ₩3M if that helps..."
(Looks desperate, makes them wonder why it was ₩4M before)

**DO:**
"Standard pricing is ₩4M/month. For a 6-month commitment, I can offer ₩3.6M/month (10% discount). That's competitive and allows us to deliver the quality you deserve."
(Clear rationale, maintains value perception)

## Pricing Adjustments Over Time

### When to Raise Prices

**Client-Specific Increases:**
- After 12 months, 5-10% increase (inflation, value growth)
- When scope has crept significantly beyond original
- When client's business has grown substantially
- When our capabilities have advanced

**Market-Wide Increases:**
- Annual rate card updates (5-8% typical)
- Communicated 3 months in advance
- Existing clients: 3-6 month grace period

**How to Communicate:**
"As you know, we've been partnered for over a year. Our rates are increasing 8% for new clients in 2026. For valued partners like you, we're offering a 6-month grace period before your rate adjusts. Your new rate starting July would be ₩3.8M (from ₩3.5M). Let's discuss if the current scope still makes sense."

### When to Lower Prices

**Rarely the Answer:**
- Almost never mid-engagement (sets bad precedent)
- Only if scope is genuinely reduced
- Only if we made mistake in original scoping

**Better Alternatives to Lowering Price:**
- Add value (more deliverables, better service)
- Restructure scope (change what's included)
- Performance model (align to outcomes)
- Longer commitment (discount for loyalty)

## Pricing Red Flags

### Walk Away When:

- Client only cares about lowest price (no value discussion)
- Scope keeps expanding without budget discussion
- "Can you just do this one thing for free?"
- Payment history is problematic (late pays, disputes)
- Unrealistic expectations for budget ("₩500K for full rebrand")
- Disrespectful of your expertise or time
- "Exposure" or "portfolio piece" instead of pay
- Equity-only for unproven startup
- Extreme rush with no premium offered

### Warning Signs:

- Multiple competitive bids (price shopping)
- Vague scope ("we'll figure it out")
- Budget "not approved yet" but want to start
- "Just send me your rate card" (no context)
- Friend-of-friend introductions with no vetting
- Asking for discount before even seeing proposal

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring Effect

**Strategy:**
Present highest option first
- Makes lower options seem more reasonable
- Example: Show ₩5M package, then ₩3.5M seems affordable

**In Practice:**
"Our comprehensive package is ₩5M/month [anchor high]. Most clients find that our ₩3.5M package [target] delivers everything they need."

### Decoy Pricing

**Strategy:**
Create option specifically to make another look good

**Example:**
- Option A: ₩5M (comprehensive, maybe overkill)
- Option B: ₩3.5M (recommended, perfect)
- Option C: ₩2M (bare minimum, seems insufficient)

**Result:**
Most choose Option B (as intended)

### Price-Quality Correlation

**Reality:**
People associate higher prices with higher quality

**Implication:**
- Too cheap = perceived as low quality
- Appropriate premium = perceived as expert
- Sweet spot = high enough to be credible, not so high they can't afford

**Our Positioning:**
- Premium, not luxury (not the most expensive, but not cheap)
- Justified by proven frameworks and track record
- "You get what you pay for" psychology works for us

### Payment Terms Psychology

**₩3,500,000/month** feels expensive

**₩117,000/day** feels reasonable

**Same price, different framing.**

Also:
- "Less than hiring one person" (vs. salaries)
- "₩3.5M investment for [specific outcome]" (vs. "costs ₩3.5M")
- "₩21M over 6 months to transform your brand" (vs. monthly)

**Typical rates:** ₩5-10M/month

**Our advantage:** More efficient, proven frameworks, 30-40% less expensive

### Freelancers

**Typical rates:** ₩1-3M/month (individual)

**Typical cost:** ₩4-6M/month (salary + benefits + management overhead)
**What they include:** One person's skillset, 3-6 month ramp-up
**Our advantage:** Immediate start, diverse expertise, no employment overhead

### Our Positioning

```
Freelancer -------- US -------- Traditional Agency
₩1-3M           ₩3-5M              ₩5-10M

[Individual]    [Team + Strategy]    [Big Team + Overhead]
```

We occupy the "best value" position:
- More than freelancer (but team capability)

## Key Pricing Principles (Summary)

1. **Value over cost** - Price what it's worth to them, not what it costs us
2. **Sustainable margins** - Must be profitable to deliver quality
3. **Transparent always** - Clear what's in, what's out, why this price
4. **Flexible strategically** - Different models for different needs
5. **Options empower** - Give choice, they pick what fits
6. **Walk away capable** - Not desperate for every deal
7. **Performance aligned** - Especially for e-commerce
8. **Premium positioned** - Not cheapest, best value
9. **Discount strategically** - For good reasons, not just to win
10. **Raise prices periodically** - We get better, prices should reflect

**Bottom line:** Our pricing reflects the value we deliver. It's profitable for us and fair for clients. We're not the cheapest, and that's by design. We're the best value.
