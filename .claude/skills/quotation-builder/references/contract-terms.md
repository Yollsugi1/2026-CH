# Contract Terms & Policies

Standard terms, conditions, and policies for Creatorhood engagements. Reference this when creating agreements and quotes.

---

## Standard Payment Terms

### Monthly Retainer Services

**Invoicing:**
- Invoice issued on 1st day of each month
- Covers services for that calendar month
- First month prorated if starting mid-month

**Payment:**
- Net 15 (payment due within 15 days of invoice)
- Accepted methods: Bank transfer, credit card (Stripe)
- Late payment: 2% per month fee after 30 days

**Auto-Renewal:**
- Contracts auto-renew monthly after initial term
- 30-day written notice required to cancel
- Work continues through notice period

### Project-Based Services

**Deposit:**
- 50% upfront deposit to begin work
- Secures team time and resources
- Non-refundable once work commences

**Final Payment:**
- 50% due upon project completion
- Must be paid before final files delivered
- Net 15 terms apply

**Milestone Payments (Projects >₩5M):**
- Phase 1: 40% upfront
- Phase 2: 30% at midpoint milestone
- Phase 3: 30% upon completion

### Performance-Based Services

**Monthly Reconciliation:**
- Sales data reviewed 1st week of month
- Invoice issued by 10th of month
- Payment due Net 15 (by 25th of month)

**Base + Performance:**
- Base fee invoiced monthly (normal terms)
- Performance share reconciled and invoiced after month-end
- Separate line items on invoice for clarity

**Minimum Terms:**
- 3-month minimum engagement
- Need time to prove performance model
- Early exit allowed if mutually agreed

## Scope & Deliverables

### What's Included (Standard Retainer)

**Strategy:**
- Monthly content calendar and planning
- Strategy sessions (monthly or bi-weekly per agreement)
- Performance analysis and reporting
- Ongoing optimization recommendations

**Production:**
- Agreed number of content pieces (video, photo, design)
- Copywriting for all content
- Editing and post-production
- Platform-optimized file exports

**Management:**
- Content scheduling and posting
- Basic community management (comments, DMs during business hours)
- Hashtag and keyword strategy
- Performance tracking

**Meetings:**
- Monthly strategy call (1-2 hours)
- Email and Slack support during business hours
- Bi-weekly check-ins (if full-service package)

### What's NOT Included (Unless Specified)

**Additional Costs:**
- Paid advertising spend (management fee separate)
- Influencer or creator fees
- Stock footage or music licensing (commercial use)
- Location rental fees or permits
- Professional models or talent fees (beyond basic)
- Travel outside Seoul metro area
- Rush fees (unless pre-agreed)
- Multiple rounds of revisions beyond agreed limit

**Out of Scope:**
- Website development or maintenance
- E-commerce platform work
- Email marketing campaigns (separate service)
- Graphic design unrelated to social content
- Event planning or coordination (beyond coverage)
- PR or media relations
- Customer service or support responses

**Technical/Platform:**
- Social media advertising account setup
- Website hosting or domain costs
- Analytics platform subscriptions (Sprout Social, etc.)
- Email service provider costs
- Other third-party tool subscriptions

### Revision Policy

**Standard Revisions Included:**
- Shortform video: 2 rounds of revisions
- Longform video: 3 rounds of revisions
- Photography: 2 rounds of edits
- Design work: 2 rounds of revisions
- Copy: 2 rounds of edits

**What Counts as a Revision:**
- Changes to existing work based on feedback
- Adjustments within original scope
- Refinements to better meet goals

**What's NOT a Revision (Additional Cost):**
- Complete creative direction change
- New concepts after approval
- Scope expansion ("Can we also add...")
- Changes after final approval
- Feedback from new stakeholders not in original process

**Excessive Revisions:**
- Beyond included rounds: ₩200K-500K per additional round
- Or: Move to hourly rate (₩80-120K/hour)

## Cancellation & Termination

### Client-Initiated Cancellation

**During Initial Term:**
- 30-day written notice required
- Client pays for current month + notice period
- No refund on work already completed
- Files delivered for work completed through notice period

**After Initial Term (Month-to-Month):**
- 30-day written notice required
- Client pays through notice period
- Continues on month-to-month basis until cancellation

**Immediate Termination:**
- By mutual agreement only
- Client pays for work completed
- Pro-rated payment for partial month
- No penalty if mutually agreed

### Creatorhood-Initiated Termination

**For Cause:**
- Non-payment (30+ days late after reminders)
- Breach of agreement
- Scope creep without compensation discussion
- Disrespectful or abusive behavior
- Immediate termination, client pays for work completed

**For Business Reasons:**
- 60-day notice (more than client's 30-day)
- Help with transition to new partner
- Complete work through notice period
- No penalty to client

### Pause or Hiatus

**Temporary Pause (Not Termination):**
- Available for monthly retainers
- Minimum 1 month pause, maximum 3 months
- No fee during pause
- Resume at current rates (no rate increase during pause)
- 30-day notice to resume

**When It Makes Sense:**
- Seasonal businesses (quiet periods)
- Company transitions
- Budget reallocation (temporary)
- Planned hiatus (sabbatical, etc.)

## Late Payment & Collections

### Late Payment Process

**15 Days Past Due:**
- Friendly reminder email
- "Payment was due on [date], just checking it didn't get missed"

**30 Days Past Due:**
- Formal notice email
- Work may be paused
- Late fee applied (2% per month)

**45 Days Past Due:**
- Work stopped
- Account sent to collections
- Legal action may be pursued
- Client responsible for collection costs

### Work Stoppage

**When We Stop Work:**
- After 30 days non-payment
- Immediate if check bounces
- If pattern of late payments continues

**What Happens:**
- No new content created
- Content scheduled continues (for 30 days)
- No strategy or support
- Resume upon payment

## Insurance & Liability

### Our Insurance

**Coverage:**
- General liability insurance (₩200M coverage)
- Professional indemnity insurance
- Equipment insurance
- Certificates available upon request

### Liability Limits

**We're Liable For:**
- Work we produce meeting agreed standards
- Meeting deadlines (or communicating delays)
- Protecting confidential information
- Professional service delivery

**We're NOT Liable For:**
- Results or outcomes (we can't guarantee sales/virality)
- Third-party platform changes (algorithm, policies)
- Client's business decisions
- Events beyond our control (platform shutdowns, etc.)

**Liability Cap:**
- Maximum liability: Total fees paid in prior 6 months
- No liability for indirect or consequential damages
- Mutual indemnification for each party's actions

## Force Majeure

### Excused Performance

**Events Beyond Control:**
- Natural disasters
- Pandemic or public health emergency
- War or terrorism
- Government restrictions
- Platform shutdowns or major outages

**What Happens:**
- Timeline adjusted as needed
- No penalty for delays due to force majeure
- Both parties work in good faith to mitigate
- Extended force majeure (>90 days) allows either party to terminate

**Minor Adjustments:**
- Within existing deliverable count

**Major Changes:**
- Additional deliverables or services
- Platform additions
- Significant timeline changes
- Require written amendment and updated pricing

### Price Changes

**Standard Increases:**
- Annual rate adjustments (5-10% typical)
- 90-day notice to existing clients

**Mid-Contract Increases:**
- Only if scope has significantly increased
- Mutual discussion and agreement required

## Dispute Resolution

### Resolution Process

**Step 1: Direct Discussion**
- Most issues resolved by conversation
- Both parties act in good faith
- Seek win-win solutions

**Step 2: Mediation**
- If direct discussion fails
- Neutral third-party mediator
- Costs split 50/50
- Non-binding but encouraged

**Step 3: Arbitration**
- If mediation fails
- Binding arbitration
- Seoul, South Korea jurisdiction
- Arbitrator's decision final

**Legal Action (Last Resort):**
- After arbitration or if arbitration refused
- Courts in Seoul, South Korea
- Prevailing party recovers legal costs

## Red Flags in Client Terms

### Watch Out For:

**Payment:**
- Net 60+ (too long, cash flow issue)
- "We'll pay after we get paid" (your risk becomes their cash flow)
- No defined payment schedule
- "Milestone payments TBD" (undefined)

**Scope:**
- "Additional services as needed" (unlimited scope creep)
- "Unlimited revisions" (can't deliver quality)
- No clear deliverables (sets us up to fail)
- "We'll figure it out as we go" (recipe for disputes)

**Rights:**
- Client owns all work including drafts (we can't show portfolio)
- Lifetime exclusivity (can't work with anyone in industry)
- No credit or attribution allowed
- Work-for-hire with no portfolio rights

**Liability:**
- Unlimited liability (unsustainable risk)
- No liability cap (one mistake = bankruptcy)
- Guarantee of results (we can't control outcomes)
- "You're responsible for everything" (even third-parties)

**Negotiation Points:**
When you see these, push back or walk away. We're not desperate.

**Remember:** Good contracts protect both parties, set clear expectations, and create foundation for successful partnerships. Don't rush into agreements without reviewing terms carefully.
