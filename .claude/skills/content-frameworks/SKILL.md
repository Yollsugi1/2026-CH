## Core Framework: A-B-C Method (Yoon Sung-won)

The foundation of Creatorhood's content planning. Every compelling content piece has three elements:

### A (주체 설정) - Subject Setting
**Who is doing the action?**

Not just "a doctor" → **"A doctor who has worked in ER for 30 years"**

The subject needs **맥락 (context)**:
- Background that makes them special
- Credentials or experience
- Specific perspective or position

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Is the subject special or interesting?
- ✓ Is the context clear?
- ✓ Would viewers care about this person?

### B (행위 설정) - Action Setting  
**What are they doing?**

Not just "cooking" → **"Cooking a meal under 5,000 won in 3 minutes"**

The action needs **특별함 (something special)**:
- Constraints (time, budget, materials)
- Unusual approach or method
- Unexpected combination

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Is it relatable yet special?
- ✓ Is it visually interesting?
- ✓ Does it clash with or enhance A?

### C (감상/이유) - Emotion/Reason
**So what? Why should I watch?**

The payoff:
- Core emotion viewers should feel
- Reason to keep watching
- Expected reaction or takeaway

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Does this answer "그래서 뭐?" (So what?)
- ✓ Is the emotion clear?
- ✓ Would viewers want to share this?

## Quick Formula

```
A (Special Subject) + B (Interesting Action) = C (Clear Payoff)
```

**Example:**
- A: "30-year ER doctor"
- B: "Reviews home first-aid kits"
- C: "Learn what actually saves lives vs. marketing hype"

## Content Planning Process

### Phase 0: Ideation Level
Start with rough ideas from:
- **Daily observations** - What did you notice today?
- **Trend analysis** - What's working on platforms?
- **Reference study** - What can we learn from successful content?

See `references/ideation-process.md` for full methodology.

### Phase 1: A-B-C Framework Application
Apply the framework to validate the idea:
1. Define A (subject + context)
2. Define B (action + what makes it special)
3. Define C (emotional payoff + why watch)

See `references/abc-framework-detailed.md` for examples and exercises.

### Phase 2: Feasibility Check
Validate against reality:
- **Connection to daily life** - Does this relate to real experience?
- **Social context** - Is this relevant to current issues/trends?
- **Timeliness** - Is this the right moment for this content?
- **Target audience** - Who will care about this?

### Phase 3: Structure Planning
For video content, plan **3-4 key moments**:
- Each moment should have its own hook
- Longform = multiple shortform moments strung together
- Identify climax moment for thumbnail/title

### Phase 4: Validation Questions
Answer these immediately:
1. **One-line pitch (A+B=C)**: Can you explain it in one sentence?
2. **Why watch?**: What's the compelling reason?
3. **Who watches?**: Can you name the target?
4. **Thumbnail?**: What's the visual hook?
5. **Title?**: How do you capture attention?

If you can't answer all 5, the concept needs more work.

## Common Pitfalls

❌ **Both A and B are generic**
- "A teacher teaches math" → No hook

❌ **Both A and B are too special**
- "Olympic gold medalist performs surgery" → Too disconnected from reality

❌ **C is unclear**
- Viewers don't know what emotion to feel or why to care

❌ **Target too narrow**
- Only a specific subgroup can understand

❌ **Disconnected from reality**
- No connection to daily life or current issues

## Examples

### Example 1: CH MAG Fitness Content
**User:** "Plan a video about home workouts"

**Apply A-B-C:**
- A: "Former Olympic trainer" (context: elite credentials)
- B: "Demonstrates 5-minute apartment workout with no equipment" (special: time constraint, no equipment)
- C: "Get Olympic-level fitness advice that actually works in real life" (payoff: elite expertise made accessible)

**One-line:** "Former Olympic trainer shows 5-minute no-equipment apartment workout"

### Example 2: Brand Content (Kuksundang)
**User:** "Plan makgeolli content for Kuksundang"

**Apply A-B-C:**
- A: "Traditional makgeolli brewery owner" (context: heritage, expertise)
- B: "Explains why cheap vs expensive makgeolli taste different" (special: myth-busting)
- C: "Understand what you're actually paying for" (payoff: informed purchasing)

**One-line:** "Brewery owner reveals the truth about makgeolli pricing"

## Troubleshooting

**Q: The idea feels flat even after applying A-B-C**
→ Check if both A and B are too generic OR too special. One should ground it, one should elevate it.

**Q: Can't identify the C (payoff)**
→ Ask "그래서 뭐?" (So what?) repeatedly until you hit emotion or insight

**Q: Target audience unclear**
→ Go back to daily observations - who actually experiences this situation?

**Q: Idea doesn't connect to brand**
→ Start with brand context, then apply A-B-C within that constraint
