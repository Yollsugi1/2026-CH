# A-B-C Framework Quick Reference

One-page cheat sheet for content planning.

---

## The Formula

```
A (Special Subject) + B (Interesting Action) = C (Clear Payoff)
```

---

## A (주체) - Subject + Context

**Not**: "A doctor"  
**Yes**: "A doctor who worked in ER for 30 years"

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Is the subject special?
- ✓ Is the context clear?
- ✓ Would viewers care?

**Types of Context:**
- Experience (years, background, specialization)
- Position (role, access, perspective)
- Situation (circumstance, challenge, transformation)

---

## B (행위) - Action + Constraint

**Not**: "Cooks food"  
**Yes**: "Cooks 5-star meal with gas station ingredients"

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Is it relatable yet special?
- ✓ Is it visually interesting?
- ✓ Does it clash with or enhance A?

**Types of Constraints:**
- Resource (time, budget, materials)
- Challenge (unusual method, difficulty)
- Comparison (cheap vs expensive, real vs fake)

---

## C (감상) - Emotion + Reason

**Not**: "It's educational"  
**Yes**: "Feel validated that experts struggle too"

**Checklist:**
- ✓ Does this answer "그래서 뭐?" (So what?)
- ✓ Is the emotion clear?
- ✓ Would viewers share this?

**Types of Payoffs:**
- Validation ("I knew it!")
- Discovery ("Never thought of that!")
- Aspiration ("I want that!")
- Schadenfreude ("Not just me!")
- Justice ("Finally, someone said it!")
- Practical ("I can use this!")

---

## Validation Questions

Answer these immediately:

1. **One-line pitch**: Can you explain A+B=C in one sentence?
2. **Why watch?**: What's the compelling reason?
3. **Who watches?**: Can you name the target?
4. **Thumbnail?**: What's the visual hook?
5. **Title?**: How do you capture attention?

**If you can't answer all 5, go back to A-B-C.**

---

## Common Pitfalls

❌ Both A and B are generic
❌ Both A and B are too special  
❌ C is unclear or too broad
❌ Target audience too narrow
❌ Disconnected from reality

---

## A-B Dynamics

**Reinforcing**: A enhances B
- Professional race car driver teaches defensive driving

**Contrasting**: A clashes with B
- Minimalist spends $10,000 in one day

**Revealing**: B shows hidden side of A
- Tough drill sergeant talks about skincare

---

## Quick Formula Templates

**Expertise Made Accessible:**
"[Expert A] shows [complex task B] in [simple way] to [democratize knowledge C]"

**Myth-Busting:**
"[Insider A] reveals truth about [common belief B] to [inform decisions C]"

**Unexpected Contrast:**
"[Surprising A] does [opposite activity B] to [show humanity C]"

**Resource Challenge:**
"[Skilled A] attempts [task B] with [severe constraint] to [test assumptions C]"

**Comparison:**
"[Expert A] compares [cheap vs expensive B] to [guide spending C]"

---

## Series Scaling

**Vary A**: Different experts, same format
**Vary B**: Same expert, different scenarios
**Vary C**: Same A-B, different takeaways

---

## Emergency Fixes

**Concept feels flat?**
→ Add context to A OR constraint to B

**Target unclear?**
→ Broaden A, universalize B

**Feels like clickbait?**
→ Realign C with what A-B actually delivers

---

## Remember

**One grounds it, one elevates it.**

If both are ordinary → boring  
If both are extraordinary → disconnected  
**If one grounds + one elevates → compelling**
