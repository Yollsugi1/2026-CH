## Usage Notes

This template is designed to be filled out progressively:

**Phase 0**: Ideation Level
- Collect raw ideas from daily life, trends, and references
- Don't filter too much at this stage

**Phase 1**: A-B-C Framework
- Force yourself to clearly define A, B, and C
- Use the checkpoints to validate each element

**Phase 2**: Feasibility & Structure
- Check if the idea connects to reality
- Plan out key moments (3-4 hooks)

**Phase 3**: Validation
- Answer the 5 key questions
- If you can't answer all 5, go back to A-B-C

**Phase 4**: Series Thinking (optional)
- Consider if this can become a repeatable format
- Plan how to scale or vary the concept

## Tips

- **Don't skip Phase 0**: The best ideas often come from simple daily observations
- **Be specific in A**: "A teacher" is too broad. "A high school math teacher who uses K-pop lyrics to teach calculus" is much better
- **Make B actionable**: Viewers should be able to visualize what's happening
- **C should be emotional**: "Learn something" is weak. "Feel vindicated about your instincts" is stronger
- **Use the checkpoints**: They're there to catch common mistakes early
